# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

## Introduction
UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in which attackers trick users into sending money or revealing sensitive banking details. This type of fraud has increased significantly with the rise of digital payments in India.

## How the Fraud Happens
Typically, the fraud begins when a victim receives a call, message, or link from someone pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or buyer. The attacker gains the victim’s trust and asks them to approve a "request" or scan a QR code. In many cases, the victim is told they will receive money, but in reality, they are authorizing a payment. Once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account.

## Targeted Victims
Students, elderly people, and individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems are the most common targets. Fraudsters take advantage of urgency, confusion, or lack of awareness to carry out the scam successfully.

## Consequences
The consequences can be serious, including financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in digital platforms. Since UPI transactions are instant, recovering money is often difficult.

## Conclusion
This case highlights the importance of digital literacy and awareness. Users should never share their UPI PIN, avoid unknown links, and always verify payment requests carefully to stay safe online.
